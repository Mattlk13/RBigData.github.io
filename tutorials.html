Introductory Resources


pbdDEMO

One of the best resources we have for pbdR is the pbdDEMO package and its vignette. Whether you are an experienced parallel programmer or a complete novice, this will get you up to speed on MPI, running in batch, the SPMD paradigm, and utilizing the pbdR tools.

HPSC pbdR Tutorials

Our sister site, High Performance Statistical Computing (HPSC), has some excellent, real world tutorials using the well-known Iris data set. This is a small data set, and is not meant to be a demonstration of pbdR's scaling prowess. Instead, this is a way to see how you might take a serial code and move it over to using our pbdR tools.

The current tutorials available are:

  • Clustering
  • Bootstrapping

Currently the Iris tutorial above is used in the "Introducing R" tutorial slides.

Introductory R

Most of the materials on this website are either programming language agnostic, or assume you already know and love R. If you are a complete beginner to using R, there are many great resources available to you. In no particular order, here are a few you may find useful:

  • Introduction to Probability and Statistics Using R by G. Jay Kerns.
  • An Introduction to R by the R Core Team.
  • The R Inferno by Patrick Burns.
  • Norm Matloff's R for Programmers (which are an early draft of his excellent book The Art of R Programming.


Introduction to Parallel Computing

If you want a very thorough, general introduction to parallel computing, then the article Introduction to Parallel Computing by Blaise Barney of LLNL is a great place to start. Neither the author nor the content of that article are affiliated with team pbdR, but it is a great place to start.
